# Taste skill guidelines

These guidelines define the visual taste standard for this project.

## 1. Overall feeling

- Calm, premium, and welcoming
- Contemporary, but not sterile or overly tech-forward
- Organic and tactile rather than rigid or corporate
- Confident enough to feel curated, simple enough to feel local

## 2. Composition

- Build layouts with a strong hero, then alternate dense and breathable sections
- Use asymmetry on desktop, but keep mobile reading order obvious
- Prefer 2–4 large visual moves per section rather than many small ones
- Let imagery lead; text should frame the story, not overpower it

## 3. Shape language

- Favor rounded corners, arches, pills, and soft organic silhouettes
- Mix one dominant large shape with smaller supporting shapes
- Avoid sharp, mechanical, or highly angular layouts unless used very sparingly

## 4. Color direction

- Base palette: sun-washed neutrals, cactus greens, clay and terracotta accents
- Use dark green for depth and grounding
- Use warm off-white as the default canvas
- Use accent colors sparingly so the page still feels refined

## 5. Typography

- Use a high-contrast serif for emotional headlines
- Use a clean sans-serif for body text, labels, navigation, and UI
- Headlines should feel editorial and generous
- Supporting text should stay short, readable, and lightly spaced

## 6. Imagery

- Prioritize human, product, and detail photography together
- Crop images intentionally so they feel designed, not simply inserted
- Combine at least one lifestyle image with one detail/product image in major sections
- Keep image treatment natural; avoid heavy filters or oversaturation

## 7. Interaction

- Motion should be subtle and soft
- Hover states should reinforce polish, not call attention to themselves
- Buttons and links should feel tactile and easy to scan

## 8. Restraint rules

- Do not overcrowd the page with too many decorative effects
- Do not use more than one dominant accent color per section
- Do not flatten hierarchy by making everything equally bold
- Do not sacrifice accessibility for style

## 9. Accessibility baseline

- Keep text contrast strong against every background
- Maintain clear focus and hover affordances
- Preserve legible type sizes on mobile
- Ensure key actions are visible without relying on color alone
